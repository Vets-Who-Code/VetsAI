Job Code: 26V

Description:
Supervises, installs and performs direct and general support and depot level maintenance on strategic communications systems.

Supervises the performance of direct support, general support, and depot level maintenance in strategic communications systems radio, multiplex, and associated ancillary equipment; makes work assignments of subordinates; corrects faulty maintenance practices; schedules maintenance; ensures that liaison is maintained between operations and maintenance personnel; coordinates installation of new or replacement equipment; assists in inspection of maintenance activities, and initiates appropriate corrective actions; reviews, consolidates, prepares and maintains administrative reports and files in accordance with applicable publications and accepted procedures; assists subordinate personnel in diagnosis of unusual or complex maintenance problems; supervises subordinate personnel in the performance of maintenance activities; informs superiors on maintenance activities; supervises the performance of direct support, general support and depot level maintenance, calibration, and installation of communications-electronic equipment; determines requirements, assigns duties and coordinates the activities of subordinate personnel; advises maintenance officer or unit commander on maintenance activities; assists command and staff in continuous appraisal of communications-electronic equipment maintenance, calibration, and installation, operations, and training; provides guidance to subordinate personnel; applies policies and procedures for facility/unit/station management; supervises the duties performed by subordinate personnel engages in installation/maintenance; determines effectiveness of performance to meet workload demands.

Performs direct support, general support and depot level maintenance on strategic communications systems radio, multiplex and associated ancillary equipment; installs, inspects, tests, services, adjusts, aligns, calibrates, and repairs strategic communications systems radio, multiplex and ancillary equipment; performs preventive maintenance and nonscheduled corrective maintenance on microwave and troposcatter transmitter and receiver equipment, fixed station carrier and multiplex equipment, and associated communications security devices; maintains and repairs AC/DC power supplies, patch panels, jack assemblies, rectifiers and DC battery plants; performs operator functions on AC power generators; prepares periodic and special propagation production charts and data; utilizes oscilloscopes, signal generators, frequency counters, power meters and special test equipment when maintaining and repairing strategic communications systems radio, multiplex and associated ancillary equipment; complies with regulations concerning transmission security and uses electronic countermeasure terms and definitions; analyzes operation of electronic time division and pulse code modulation equipment of facilitate troubleshooting and repair; performs malfunction diagnostic procedures when troubleshooting equipment; localizes C9 malfunctions, determines causes and replaces defective parts or components; tests and tunes strategic communications system equipment; adjust magic Tees, diplexers and duplexers in the maintenance and repair of microwave and troposcatter radio equipment; aligns high power amplifiers, parametric amplifiers and receivers positions and assembles new and replacement equipment, erects and orients antenna on contingency systems; interconnects components and power units; starts and adjusts power units to ensure delivery of power at prescribed readings; checks and adjusts carrier and multiplex equipment to maintain efficiency of circuits and to ensure proper functioning of equipment; performs maintenance tasks utilizing applicable and appropriate publications and standards; employs hand tools and test equipment to perform the required duties; performs direct support, general support and depot level maintenance on strategic communications systems radio, multiplex and associated ancillary equipment; trains complex concepts, instructs and demonstrates correct maintenance procedures utilizing special hand tools and test equipment; localizes malfunctions, determines causes of malfunctions and the extent of repairs or replacement of parts required and fabricates simple parts not requiring precision tooling; maintains hand tools, testing devices and test equipment in proper operating condition; interprets tele-communications; service Orders to activate, change and deactivate circuits, to include pre-activation, testing, reporting and recording; stocks and requisitions repair parts; supervises the performance of direct support, general support and depot level maintenance on strategic communications systems radio, multiplex, and associated ancillary equipment; advises superiors and subordinates on maintenance problems; prepares and maintains equipment maintenance records in accordance with applicable publications and accepted procedures; establishes liaison with supported and supporting activities; advises superiors of equipment maintenance situations; provides guidance to subordinates regarding maintenance schedules; coordinates maintenance problems, schedules and hazardous conditions with defense communications technical control facilities; exercises technical direction, coordination, and supervision over the activation, deactivation and management of transmission links, super groups, groups, and channels in accordance with appropriate directives; prepares and maintains records pertaining to prescribed load lists and authorized stockage lists.

Supervises the performance of direct support, general support and depot level maintenance, calibration, and installation of communications-electronic equipment; determines requirements, assigns duties and coordinates the activities of subordinate personnel; advises maintenance officer or unit commander on maintenance activities; assists command and staff in continuous appraisal of communications-electronic equipment maintenance, calibration, and installation, operations, and training; provides guidance to subordinate personnel; applies policies and procedures for facility, unit, station management; supervises the duties performed by subordinate personnel engages in installation/maintenance; determines effectiveness of performance to meet workload demands; provides advice and makes, recommendations to the commander aid staff on all matters pertain to enlisted personnel and their families; accompanies the commander on inspections and visits and at ceremonies; assists in inspection of command activities facilities aid personnel as prescribed by the commander; holds First Sergeants' or Sergeants' Major call to announce information and instructions; insures that newly assigned enlisted personnel are instructed in military courtesy, customs, of the service, and command regulations or policies; provides counsel and guidance to NCOs and other enlisted personnel of the command; inspects duties performed by subordinate NCOs; notes discrepancies and initiates appropriate corrective action; assists in reception of visitors to the command; sits as president or, member of command promotion board for NCOs, as authorized by regulations; per-forms other duties prescribed by, the commander.
