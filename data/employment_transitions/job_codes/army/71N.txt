Job Code: 71N

Description:
Supervises, coordinates and controls movement of personnel and cargo by air, rail, highway and water.

Must be able to supervise personnel performing duties of Motor Transport Operator (64C40), Traffic Management Coordinator (71N40) or Terminal Operations Coordinator (57H40); serves as principal NCO in transportation company or comparable organization; operations section of transportation battalion or higher level headquarters; transportation special staff section in division or higher level headquarters; or movement control office; prepares, reproduces and distributes operation reports; edits and prepares tactical plans and training material; assists in coordination and implementation of operations, training programs, administrative matters and communication activities; maintains records of strength, location and employment of subordinate transportation activities; coordinates transportation activities with subordinate and serviced activities; assists in providing staff supervision, policy and guidance relating to movements of personnel and cargo by air, rail, motor transport and water; consolidates, prepares reviews and processes regular and special reports of command transportation activities; performs principal noncommissioned officer duties associates with SQI M; provides advice and makes, recommendations to the commander aid staff on all matters pertain to enlisted personnel and their families; accompanies the commander on inspections and visits and at ceremonies; assists in inspection of command activities facilities aid personnel as prescribed by the commander; holds first sergeants' or sergeants' major call to announce information and instructions; insures that newly assigned enlisted personnel are instructed in military courtesy, customs, of the service, and command regulations or policies; provides counsel and guidance to NCOs and other enlisted personnel of the command; inspects duties performed by subordinate NCOs; notes discrepancies and initiates appropriate corrective action; assists in reception of visitors to the command; sits as president or, member of command promotion board for NCOs, as authorized by regulations; performs other duties prescribed by, the commander.

Supervises personnel and cargo movement operations and training; supervises cargo documentation unit or movement control office; supervises freight, passenger, and personal property movement function at installation level; assists in providing transportation special staff supervision in commands at all levels; reviews and verifies accuracy of all applicable movement control documentation; coordinates and supervises technical training programs; provides technical inspections instructions to personal property sections/divisions as directed or requested; plans and directs training management, and evaluates results; evaluates recommendations concerning selection of sites for depots, truck terminals, railheads, beachheads, airheads and inland waterway terminals; serves as staff advisor by determining transportation capabilities and limitations of subordinates units and technical problems related to movement control activities of assigned subordinate sections and augmenting teams; determines transportation capabilities of area units; develops movement plans and programs; performs as staff noncommissioned officer in military traffic management agencies; must be able to supervise personnel performing duties of Motor Transport Operator (64C40), Traffic Management Coordinator (71N40) or Terminal Operations Coordinator (57H40); serves as principal NCO in transportation company or comparable organization; operations section of transportation battalion or higher level headquarters; transportation special staff section in division or higher level headquarters; or movement control office; prepares, reproduces and distributes operation reports; edits and prepares tactical plans and training material; assists in coordination and implementation of operations, training programs, administrative matters and communication activities; maintains records of strength, location and employment of subordinate transportation activities; coordinates transportation activities with subordinate and serviced activities; assists in providing staff supervision, policy and guidance relating to movements of personnel and cargo by air, rail, motor transport and water; consolidates, prepares reviews and processes regular and special reports of command transportation activities; performs principal noncommissioned officer duties associates with SQI M.

Performs movement of personnel and cargo by air, rail, highway and water; assists in the coordination and allocation of transport capability to meet valid movement requirements; gathers statistical data for use in preparation of movement reports; insures that cargo shipments are properly labeled and marked; prepares entries on Government Bills of Lading (GBL) Freight Warrants, Transportation Requests (GTR), Military Transportation Authorizations (MTA), Airline Service Requests, related passenger) movement forms, message forms and routine office correspondence; performs miscellaneous administrative duties such as posting changes to transportation regulations, labeling of file folders and guides; assists in preparation of documentation for shipment of personal property; operates automated data terminals and word processing to prepare movement documentation; movement of personnel and cargo by air, rail, highway and water; performs duties shown in preceding level of skill and provides technical guidance to lower graded personnel in accomplishment of those duties; advises Military and DOD Civilians of JTR travel entitlements; advises passengers on use of transportation documentation; assist in preparation of documentation for shipment of personal property; prepares Government Transportation Request (GTR), MAC Transportation Authorization MTA, Airline Service Request and related passenger movement forms; prepares passenger travel itineraries; assist the trainer in providing technical training to lower graded personnel; supervises movement of personnel and cargo; plans, directs and controls operation of subordinate personnel; supervises cargo documentation unit or movement control office; supervises operation of movement control team, cargo documentation and port transportation section or office; supervises diversion, reconsignment, and transfer of personnel and cargo; instructs and supervises subordinate personnel in transportation movement, work techniques and procedures; reviews and consolidates and prepares technical, personnel and administrative reports covering transportation movement operations; edits and consolidates personal property documentation; prepares unit movement reports using applicable forms and regulations; reviews records, reports and DOD contracts with civilian firms, agreements with host nations and with other military services; maintains liaison with air, rail, highway and water transportation facilities; interprets and verifies movement schedules; checks and consolidates movement requirements and prepares movement schedules; assists in planning future transportation requirements; coordinates freight and passenger movement activities.
