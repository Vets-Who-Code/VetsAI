Job Code: 51L

Description:
Installs, operates, services, and repairs conventional mechanical refrigeration equipment.

Must know principles of mechanical refrigeration, basic thermodynamics, and psychometrics as applied to refrigeration equipment; must know characteristics, chemical properties, and utilization of various types of refrigerants, to include secondary refrigerants and preparation and testing of brine solutions; must know fundamentals of electricity as applied to control circuits and devices such as thermostats, circuit breakers, solenoids, relays, and regulators; must know operating principles and organizational maintenance of gasoline engines; must know operating principles, maintenance and repair of multi-fuel forced air, duct types heaters; must know safety procedures applicable to toxic gases, pressurized components, and electricity; must know construction and function of refrigerant compressors, receivers, regulating valves, filters, condensers, evaporators, and connecting parts; must know how to leak test by use of halide and electronic testers; must know how to evacuate refrigerant, disassemble, repair, test, reassemble, purge, and recharge mechanical refrigerating systems; must know Army maintenance system, shop procedures, and repair parts procedures; must be able to repair and fabricate new lines by cutting, bending, tubing, and installing fittings by flaring or soldering; must be able to repair electric wiring in the control circuits and make repairs to electro-mechanical controls; must be able to read circuit diagrams and flow charts; must be able to interpret temperature and regulate and adjust equipment to produce required refrigeration and/or flow of refrigerant fluids, gases, water and air; must be able to use electricians, mechanics, and refrigeration hand and power tools, measuring devices, and test instruments; must be able to operate and demonstrate equipment operation to using unit personnel.
